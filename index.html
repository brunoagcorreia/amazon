Overview

After years of reduction, Amazon deforestation has increased since Dilma Roussef's impeachment in 2016.

Figures have skyrocketed this year under the extreme right-wing government of Jair Bolsonaro, an openly declared climate change denialist.

The German and Norwegian governments, the largest contributors to the Fundo Amazônia (a billionaire international fund for preservation of the Amazon), have suspended transfers unless the Brazilian government resumes measures to fight deforestation.

Methodology

The figures presented on this website are conservative estimates, using the last publicly available figures, the 12 month period between August 2017 to July 2018, when 7.900 square quilometers of the Brazilian Amazon were brought down mostly for cattle and agriculture.

The areas of New York and London have been used in order to put into perspective how much jungle is being cut down every hour.

What can be done?

There are people making a difference in the fight to stop deforestation. Join or support their cause.

Extinction Rebellion

Extinction Rebellion is a socio-political movement with the stated aim of using civil disobedience and nonviolent resistance to protest against climate breakdown, biodiversity loss, and the risk of social and ecological collapse. You can donate here.

Fridays for Future

#FridaysForFuture is a movement that began in August 2018, after 15 years old Greta Thunberg sat in front of the Swedish parliament every schoolday for three weeks, to protest against the lack of action on the climate crisis. She posted what she was doing on Instagram and Twitter and it soon went viral. You can join here.
